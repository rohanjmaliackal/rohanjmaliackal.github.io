Arthritis

Arthritis is normally referred to joint disease or joint pains. It is most common in women but frequently seen in older people. The symptoms can be mild, moderate or severe. Swelling pain, stiffness,decreased range of motion are the common symptoms of arthritis. These may stay about the same for years and progress or get worse over time.

Degenerative arthritis
Osteoarthritis is the most common among arthritis. It occurs when the cartilage, the soft cushion-like tissue , wears away causing the bones to rub against each other causing pain, swelling and stiffness. It is commonly seen in people with excess weight, family history, age, previous injury etc.

Inflammatory Arthritis
The immune system protects our body from infections. But sometimes, they can go awry and attack the organs with uncontrolled inflammation. This may damage internal organs, eyes and other parts of the body. Rheumatoid arthritis and psoriatic arthritis are examples.

Infectious Arthritis
This is triggered by infections by either a bacterium or virus or fungus.
